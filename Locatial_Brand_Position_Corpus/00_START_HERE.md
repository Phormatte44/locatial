# Locatial Brand, Position and Place-Intelligence Corpus

## Purpose

This repository is the central working corpus for Locatial's brand, positioning, editorial system, NYC bars guide, place graph, nightlife intelligence, data acquisition strategy and prototype requirements.

It is not a loose archive. It is the shared reasoning harness that Claude, Codex, designers, researchers and engineers should use before proposing, designing or building related work.

## Core thesis

**Locatial is a spatial media platform: a publishing and intelligence layer for the physical world.**

Locatial does not merely show where places are. It helps people understand what places mean, what they are good for, when they work, how they relate to one another and how broader cultural patterns emerge across geography.

## Product belief

> A place is not a location. It is a story, an experience and a set of relationships that change through time.

## Brand hierarchy

- **Category:** The spatial media platform
- **Creator proposition:** Make place your medium
- **End-user promise:** Make the world readable
- **Brand belief:** The world is the interface
- **Internal mission:** Back into the world
- **Product-expression principle:** A map is not opened. A place is revealed

## Immediate prototype

The first product stress test is an NYC nightlife guide that answers:

> What kind of night is this part of New York good for?

The prototype should show five experiential layers:

1. After work
2. Budget drinking
3. Date night
4. Sports viewing
5. Late-night energy

The map should reveal fields, clusters and corridors, then resolve into individual bars as users move closer.
