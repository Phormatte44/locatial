# Nightlife Spatial Character Layers

## Product idea

Locatial should reveal the social topography of nightlife.

A pin shows where one bar is.

A spatial layer shows what kind of night becomes more likely across an area.

## Initial layers

- After work
- Budget drinking
- Date night
- Sports viewing
- Late-night energy

## Visual forms

### Fields

Use for gradual characteristics such as price, energy, after-work suitability and date suitability.

### Corridors

Use for street-aligned behavior such as sports-bar strips, live-music routes and bar crawls.

### Clusters

Use for compact destinations such as wine-bar pockets, rooftop concentrations or dive-bar groups.

### Contours

Use for intensity and confidence without implying a hard administrative boundary.

## Scale behavior

### City

Show broad nightlife fields.

### Neighborhood

Show clusters, corridors and transitions.

### Street

Show relevant places and relative strength.

### Place

Show full identity, score, confidence, applicable time and evidence summary.

## Time sensitivity

Attributes must support:

- day of week;
- time of day;
- season;
- event conditions;
- last verified;
- confidence.

A place can be conversational at 6 p.m. and loud at 11 p.m. Both can be true.

## Composite scoring examples

### After work

- weekday 5–8 p.m. observations;
- office proximity;
- happy hour;
- group suitability;
- food;
- commuter access;
- creator and user confirmation.

### Sports viewing

- screen count;
- sports schedule;
- event-night behavior;
- supporter-group presence;
- venue programming;
- user confirmation.

### Budget

- verified prices;
- receipt evidence;
- cover charge;
- happy-hour value;
- typical spend;
- creator and user confirmation.

## Language

Formal layer names should be neutral and defensible.

Use "high-spend nightlife" rather than "bougie area."

Use "student-heavy at night" rather than "college-kid bar."
