# Data Acquisition, Evidence and Confidence

## Principle

No single provider can reliably describe cultural and experiential place qualities.

Locatial must combine multiple evidence layers.

## Source layers

### 1. Public and government data

Used for identity, legality and civic context.

Examples:

- liquor licenses;
- open map data;
- city open data;
- inspections;
- land use;
- transit;
- public events.

### 2. Commercial place services

Used for discovery, matching and discrepancy checks.

Examples:

- hours;
- phone;
- website;
- operating status;
- categories;
- provider IDs.

Provider rights must be tracked by field.

### 3. Venue-supplied facts

Venues may authoritatively provide operational facts such as hours, prices, access, programming and reservations.

Venues should not control cultural classifications.

### 4. Creator and field-research observations

Structured visits should capture:

- arrival time;
- queue;
- occupancy;
- standing versus seated;
- group-size patterns;
- sound;
- screens;
- service;
- observed prices;
- conversation difficulty;
- suitable and unsuitable occasions;
- defining observations;
- evidence photographs or receipts.

### 5. User confirmations

Ask small contextual questions after likely visits.

Examples:

- Could you talk comfortably?
- Did you wait?
- Were drinks under $20?
- Was sport shown?
- Would this work for six?
- Did it feel after-work or late-night?
- Was the description accurate?

## Confidence dimensions

### Authority

How reliable is the source for this specific claim type?

### Independence

Are the confirmations genuinely independent?

### Recency

How quickly should the claim decay?

### Specificity

Is the observation precise or vague?

## Contradiction handling

Do not average contradictions into meaninglessness.

Resolve them through context whenever possible.

Example:

- conversational early;
- loud after 10 p.m. Friday and Saturday.

## Freshness

Automated checks should detect:

- closures;
- license changes;
- broken websites;
- changed hours;
- reservation failures;
- menu changes;
- new businesses at the same site;
- source disagreement.

Human review should be triggered when identity or evidence becomes ambiguous.

## Strategic moat

Locatial should own:

- classifications;
- evidence graph;
- score logic;
- confidence;
- temporal context;
- spatial relationships.

Commodity provider data should support the system, not define it.
