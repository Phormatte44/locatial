# NYC Bars Guide Product Model

## Purpose

The NYC guide should not attempt to become a better directory of bars.

Its job is to answer:

> What will it actually feel like to go here, and is it right for this particular night?

## User decision needs

Users need to understand:

- primary reason to visit;
- occasion fit;
- room character;
- social character;
- access friction;
- realistic spend;
- defining order;
- surrounding context;
- time-based change.

## Guide organization

The guide should support three simultaneous views.

### By intention

- after work;
- date night;
- group night;
- solo drinking;
- sports viewing;
- one serious drink;
- beginning the night;
- ending the night;
- conversation;
- dancing;
- visitors;
- budget.

### By geography

- SoHo and Nolita;
- East Village;
- Lower East Side;
- Chinatown;
- West Village;
- Chelsea;
- Hell's Kitchen;
- Williamsburg;
- Greenpoint;
- Bushwick;
- Downtown Brooklyn;
- Red Hook;
- Astoria;
- Jackson Heights.

### By venue character

- cocktail;
- wine;
- dive;
- pub;
- beer;
- hotel;
- rooftop;
- listening;
- sports;
- live music;
- dance;
- restaurant bar;
- historic;
- neighborhood local;
- late night.

## Product differentiation

Google supplies coordinates and utility data.

Review sites supply volume and ratings.

Venue websites supply promotion.

Locatial should supply situational understanding, spatial context, time-based behavior and editorial confidence.
