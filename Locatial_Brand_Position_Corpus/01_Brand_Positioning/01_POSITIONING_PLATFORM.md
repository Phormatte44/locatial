# Locatial Positioning Platform

## Category

Locatial is the spatial media platform.

It is a publishing, discovery and intelligence layer for the physical world. It allows creators and users to understand places through stories, guides, collections, live context and relationships.

## The problem

Digital maps are excellent at locating things but weak at explaining what places mean, how they feel, when they work and how they belong to a wider cultural context.

Review sites reduce places to ratings. Social platforms detach recommendations from meaningful geography. Travel products frame cities as inventories. GIS products expose data without making it culturally legible.

## The opportunity

Locatial can make the physical world readable.

It can reveal:

- what a place is good for;
- who and what it relates to;
- when its character changes;
- how places form scenes, corridors and districts;
- how a person might move through them;
- how creators interpret the world through place.

## Positioning statement

For culturally curious people, creators and local experts who want to understand and communicate the physical world, Locatial is a spatial media platform that turns places into connected, readable media.

Unlike map directories, review platforms and social feeds, Locatial combines place identity, editorial interpretation, temporal context and spatial relationships into one coherent medium.

## Strategic expressions

- **Category:** The spatial media platform
- **Creator proposition:** Make place your medium
- **End-user promise:** Make the world readable
- **Brand belief:** The world is the interface
- **Internal mission:** Back into the world
- **Product principle:** A map is not opened. A place is revealed

## What Locatial is not

- A generic map app
- A travel booking product
- Instagram placed on a map
- A ratings directory
- A social discovery feed
- A GIS dashboard
- An AI wrapper around commodity place data
- A list of pins with editorial copy attached

## Emotional proposition

Locatial uses sophisticated digital systems to return attention to the physical world.

The product should make people more curious, more observant and more willing to move through real places.

## Desired perception

Locatial should feel:

- intelligent;
- calm;
- culturally alive;
- cinematic;
- useful;
- authored;
- editorial;
- spatially native;
- materially considered.

It should not feel cold, overbuilt or self-consciously luxurious.
