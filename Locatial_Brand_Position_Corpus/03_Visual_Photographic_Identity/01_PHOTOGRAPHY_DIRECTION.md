# Photography Direction

## Core photographic idea

> Photograph the life of the place, not its inventory.

The photography should feel like contemporary editorial documentary work, not venue marketing or property photography.

## Required image sequence

### 1. Threshold

Show the exterior, entrance, stair, awning or doorway at the time a person would realistically arrive.

### 2. Room in use

Show density, lighting, seating, spatial relationships and social organization. This should usually be the hero.

### 3. Human scale

Show people talking, bartenders moving, a person alone at the bar or a drink arriving. Subjects need not face the camera.

### 4. Defining detail

Show the object or material that gives the room memory: worn counter, lamp, tile, mirror, handwritten menu, jukebox, curtain or matchbook.

### 5. Defining drink in context

Show the drink inside the room, carrying the same light and atmosphere. Avoid isolated product photography.

### 6. Surrounding street

Show the block, corner or nighttime approach so the venue remains connected to the city.

## Visual qualities

- available or convincingly available light;
- readable shadow;
- natural grain;
- architectural awareness;
- honest crowd density;
- mild movement;
- candid use of space;
- restrained color treatment;
- real time-of-day conditions.

## Avoid

- empty property-listing interiors;
- ultra-wide distortion;
- overlit cocktails;
- posed groups;
- influencer portraits;
- neon-sign clichés;
- excessive shallow depth of field;
- heavy teal-orange grading;
- only venue-supplied imagery;
- daytime imagery representing nightlife;
- cropping away the surrounding city.

## Identity principle

The hero image should usually be people inhabiting the room, not a perfect drink or an empty interior.
