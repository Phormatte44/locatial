# Locatial Verbal Identity

## Voice

Locatial is observant, spatial, specific, culturally literate, lightly opinionated and unsentimental.

It behaves like an intelligent local who notices how places work, respects the reader's time and money, and understands the relationship between a venue and its surroundings.

## Core rule

> Describe evidence before making a judgment.

Weak:

> A stylish bar with an incredible atmosphere.

Better:

> Low lamps divide the narrow room into small pools of light. It feels private until the second row forms behind the stools.

## Tone characteristics

Use:

- precise sensory observation;
- useful situational judgment;
- controlled confidence;
- clear distinctions;
- mild wit when earned;
- plain language over promotional language.

Avoid:

- breathless enthusiasm;
- influencer language;
- faux-local performance;
- adjective stacking;
- generic praise;
- unsupported superlatives;
- snobbery;
- corporate product language.

## Words to avoid

- hidden gem;
- must-visit;
- iconic, unless historically justified;
- Instagrammable;
- vibrant;
- amazing vibe;
- something for everyone;
- ultimate;
- hottest;
- best in the city, unless the claim is explicitly framed and defensible.

## Brand identity through editorial behavior

The identity should emerge through what Locatial notices:

1. Signal — why the place matters
2. Threshold — how arrival feels
3. Room — how the space is organized
4. People — how it is used
5. Time — how it changes
6. Context — how it belongs to the area
7. Movement — where it leads next

Locatial begins with experience, not business biography.
