# Locatial Graph Thesis

## Definition

The Locatial graph is the relationship system that connects places to sites, operating entities, people, stories, creators, attributes, occasions, time conditions, routes, events and other places.

Latitude and longitude describe position.

The graph describes meaning.

## Core relationships

A place may connect to:

- a physical site;
- a current operating entity;
- historical occupants;
- a neighborhood;
- a street;
- nearby venues;
- audience patterns;
- occasions;
- price bands;
- creators;
- stories;
- guides;
- collections;
- drinks;
- events;
- cultural scenes;
- routes;
- before and after destinations.

## Why the graph matters

Without the graph, the map can only show isolated records.

With the graph, Locatial can answer:

- What is this place good for?
- What other places share that quality?
- When is the quality true?
- Where do those places form a cluster?
- What path connects them?
- What changes when time advances?
- Which recommendations fit a person's taste and current intent?

## Spatial projection

The nightlife heatmap is not the graph itself.

It is a projection of selected graph relationships onto geography.

Examples:

- after-work attributes projected as a spatial field;
- sports-viewing relationships projected as a corridor;
- budget bars projected as a cluster;
- date-night suitability projected as an overlapping area;
- late-night activity projected differently by hour.

## Long-term value

The proprietary asset is not raw coordinates, ratings or provider categories.

It is the evidence-backed relationship layer describing what places mean, when those meanings apply and how they connect.
